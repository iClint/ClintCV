About Me

I’m Clint — a fullstack software engineer who builds scalable, performant web applications across Angular, .NET, and Azure. I care deeply about clean architecture, user experience, and delivering real-world outcomes — without the bloat or bullshit.

My journey into tech started in the 90s, coding in BASIC on a beige-box 8086. I studied Computing Studies through high school, but life took me elsewhere. I ran a Cash-in-Transit business for over a decade before a serious accident (and a global pandemic) forced a rethink.

I pivoted fast — taking a role in Woolworths Online, where I honed my customer skills and quick thinking under pressure. My curiosity pulled me toward WooliesX, and I started building a portfolio on the side to prove I belonged. I was selected as one of 12 candidates (out of 100+) for a WooliesX pilot program into fullstack engineering. After an intense 12-week bootcamp, I landed my first official tech role.

These days I write better code, ship smarter features, and work toward scalable frontend/backend systems. I value stateless components, useful tests, and clear commit messages. I don’t do cargo cult patterns or endless meetings — I just get things done.

Outside of work, I’m usually flying simulated helicopters in MSFS, tuning my home lab, or questioning whether I really need a sixth monitor. (Spoiler: I do.)
